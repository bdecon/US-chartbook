A monthly index produced by the Federal Reserve \href{https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/g17/Current/default.htm}{shows} industrial production increased by 16.5 percent over the year ending April 2021, following an increase of 1.0 percent over the year ending March 2021. One-year growth in manufacturing production was 23.0 percent in April 2021, and manufacturing contributed 17.5 percentage points to the overall change in industrial production. Over the same period, mining subtracted 0.3 percentage point from the overall change, and electric and gas utilities contributed 0.2 percentage point. 

By market group, production of consumer goods contributed 5.9 percentage points to one-year industrial production growth in April 2021. Production of business equipment contributed 4.1 percentage points, production of nonindustrial supplies contributed 2.1 percentage points, and production of materials contributed 4.8 percentage points.
