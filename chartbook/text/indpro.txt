A monthly index produced by the Federal Reserve \href{https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/g17/Current/default.htm}{shows} industrial production decreased by 4.2 percent over the year ending February 2021, following a decrease of 2.0 percent over the year ending January 2021. One-year growth in manufacturing production was -4.1 percent in February 2021, and manufacturing subtracted 3.1 percentage points from the overall change in industrial production. Over the same period, mining subtracted 1.9 percentage points from the overall change, and electric and gas utilities contributed 1.2 percentage points. 

By market group, production of consumer goods contributed 0.3 percentage point to one-year industrial production growth in February 2021. Production of business equipment subtracted 0.7 percentage point, production of nonindustrial supplies subtracted 0.7 percentage point, and production of materials subtracted 2.9 percentage points.
