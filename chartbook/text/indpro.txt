A monthly index produced by the Federal Reserve \href{https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/g17/Current/default.htm}{shows} industrial production increased by 1.0 percent over the year ending March 2021, following a decrease of 4.8 percent over the year ending February 2021. One-year growth in manufacturing production was 3.1 percent in March 2021, and manufacturing contributed 2.4 percentage points to the overall change in industrial production. Over the same period, mining subtracted 1.2 percentage points from the overall change, and electric and gas utilities subtracted 0.0 percentage point. 

By market group, production of consumer goods contributed 1.5 percentage points to one-year industrial production growth in March 2021. Production of business equipment contributed 0.5 percentage point, production of nonindustrial supplies contributed 0.1 percentage point, and production of materials subtracted 0.9 percentage point.
