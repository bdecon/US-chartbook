A monthly index produced by the Federal Reserve \href{https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/g17/Current/default.htm}{shows} industrial production decreased by 15.3 percent over the year ending May 2020, following a decrease of 16.2 percent over the year ending April 2020. One-year growth in manufacturing production was -16.5 percent in May 2020, and manufacturing subtracted 12.2 percentage points from the overall change in industrial production. Over the same period, mining subtracted 2.1 percentage points from the overall change, and electric and gas utilities subtracted 0.9 percentage point.\\ 

By market group, production of consumer goods subtracted 3.9 percentage points from one-year industrial production growth in May 2020. Production of business equipment subtracted 2.7 percentage points, production of nonindustrial supplies subtracted 2.0 percentage points, and production of materials subtracted 6.5 percentage points.
