The Federal Reserve industrial production index \href{https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/g17/Current/default.htm}{measures} the real output of the industrial sector, which includes manufacturing, mining, and electric and gas utilities.

\textbf{Industrial production} decreased 0.4 percent over the year ending April 2024, following an increase of 0.1 percent in March. The manufacturing-only index decreased 0.5 percent over the year ending April, and subtracted 0.4 percentage point from the growth of the total index. Mining subtracted 0.2 percentage point, and utilities contributed 0.2 percentage point.

By market group, finished consumer goods subtracted 0.4 percentage point from one-year industrial production growth in April. Business equipment did not contribute, nonindustrial supplies did not contribute, and materials did not contribute.
