As of February 23, 2022, the benchmark \href{https://www.eia.gov/dnav/pet/hist/RCLC1D.htm}{futures price} for a barrel of west Texas intermediate (WTI) \textbf{crude oil} is \$92.10 (see {\color{red!80!purple}\textbf{---}}). Over the past year, this measure of oil prices increased 55.9 percent. Over the past two years, the price increased 82.0 percent. The WTI price is currently \$42 per barrel below its peak monthly average price of \$134 per barrel in June 2008.
