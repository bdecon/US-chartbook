As of 2021 Q2, there are 126.2 million total occupied housing units in the US, of which 43.6 million (34.6 percent) are rented, and 82.5 million (65.4 percent) are owner-occupied. There was an average annual net total increase of 1.8 million housing units over the year ending 2021 Q2, the result of 243,000 net new renter households and 1.5 million net new owner-occupied households. Over the year ending 2021 Q2, the total number of occupied housing units increased by 1.4 percent, compared to an increase of 2.4 percent in 2021 Q1. Owner-occupied units contributed 1.2 percent to total household formation on average over the year (see\cbox{yellow!60!orange}), compared to a contribution of 0.2 percent from rented units (see\cbox{magenta!90!blue}).
