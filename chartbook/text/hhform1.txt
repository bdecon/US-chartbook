As of 2019 Q3, there are 122.7 million total occupied housing units in the US, of which 43.2 million (35.2 percent) are rented, and 79.5 million (64.8 percent) are owner-occupied. There was a net total increase of 1.3 million housing units over the year ending 2019 Q3, the result of 33 thousand net fewer renter households and 1.4 million net new owner-occupied households. Over the year ending 2019 Q3, the total number of occupied housing units increased by 1.2 percent, compared to an increase of 1.2 percent percent in 2019 Q2. Owner-occupied units contributed 1.0 percent to total household formation on average over the year, compared to a a contribution of 0.2 percent from rented units.
