From March 2001 to the latest available month, December 2019, an additional 2.5 percent of the age 18--64 population left the labor force. The larger-than-normal population cohort born after World War II is reaching retirement age in this period. Such demographic effects explain 1.2 percent of the cumulative decrease. Additionally, young people are staying in school longer, on average, reduced the age 18--64 labor force by 1.2 percent. Disability or illness reduced the labor force by another 0.7 percent. Less retirement among those age 18--64 increased the labor force by 0.8 percent.
