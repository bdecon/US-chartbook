In the latest data, covering January 2023, 62.4 percent of people age 16 and older are in the labor force (see {\color{green!70!blue}\textbf{---}}), compared to 62.3 percent in December 2022 and 62.2 percent in November 2022. In February 2020, when US confirmed cases of COVID-19 were still low, the labor force participation rate was 63.3 percent.

In January 2023, 67.9 percent of men age 16 and older are in the labor force (see {\color{blue!90!cyan}\textbf{---}}), compared to 57.0 percent of women (see {\color{orange!90!red}\textbf{---}}). Since February 2020, labor force participation has decreased 1.3 percentage points among men, and decreased 0.8 percentage point among women.
