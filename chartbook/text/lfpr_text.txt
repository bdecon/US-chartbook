As of June 2024, 62.6 percent of those aged 16 and over are part of the labor force (see {\color{green!80!blue!60!black}\textbf{---}}), following 62.5 percent in May and 62.7 percent in April. Pre-pandemic, in February 2020, the rate stood at 63.3 percent.

In June 2024, 68.1 percent of men age 16 and older are in the labor force (see {\color{blue!70!black}\textbf{---}}), compared to 57.3 percent of women (see {\color{red!80!black}\textbf{---}}). Since February 2020, labor force participation has decreased 1.1 percentage points among men, and decreased 0.6 percentage point among women.
