In the latest data, covering February 2023, 62.5 percent of people age 16 and older are in the labor force (see {\color{green!70!blue}\textbf{---}}), compared to 62.4 percent in January and 62.3 percent in December 2022. In February 2020, when US confirmed cases of COVID-19 were still low, the labor force participation rate was 63.3 percent.

In February 2023, 68.0 percent of men age 16 and older are in the labor force (see {\color{blue!90!cyan}\textbf{---}}), compared to 57.2 percent of women (see {\color{orange!90!red}\textbf{---}}). Since February 2020, labor force participation has decreased 1.2 percentage points among men, and decreased 0.6 percentage point among women.
