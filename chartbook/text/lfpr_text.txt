In the latest data, covering January 2022, 62.2 percent of people age 16 and older are in the labor force (see {\color{green!70!blue}\textbf{---}}), compared to 61.9 percent in December 2021 and 61.9 percent in November 2021. In February 2020, when US confirmed cases of COVID-19 were still low, the labor force participation rate was 63.4 percent.

In January 2022, 67.9 percent of men age 16+ are in the labor force (see {\color{blue!90!cyan}\textbf{---}}), compared to 56.8 percent of women (see {\color{orange!90!red}\textbf{---}}). Since February 2020, labor force participation has decreased 1.4 percentage points among men, and decreased one percentage point among women.
