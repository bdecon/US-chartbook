Over the week ending July 24, 2021, there were 95,200 initial UI \href{https://oui.doleta.gov/unemploy/DataDashboard.asp}{claims} under the Pandemic Unemployment Assistance (PUA) program (see {\color{blue!50!purple!80!black}\textbf{---}}), compared to 109,900 during the prior week, and an average of 100,500 initial claims per week over the past four weeks. 

Federal program continuing claims total 9,480,045 in July 17, 2021 (see {\color{green!50!blue}\textbf{---}}). These include both claims under the PUA program and claims under the Pandemic Emergency Unemployment Compensation (PEUC) program. Combining federal program claims with state program claims indicates there are a total of 12.7 million insured unemployed persons during the week ending July 17, 2021, compared to 14.0 million one month prior, during the week ending June 26, 2021.
