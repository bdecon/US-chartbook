Over the week ending February 6, 2021, there were 334,500 initial UI \href{https://oui.doleta.gov/unemploy/DataDashboard.asp}{claims} under the Pandemic Unemployment Assistance (PUA) program (see {\color{blue!50!purple!80!black}\textbf{---}}), compared to 369,000 during the prior week, and an average of 381,700 initial claims per week over the past four weeks. 

Federal program continuing claims total 12,720,521 in January 30, 2021 (see {\color{green!50!blue}\textbf{---}}). These include both claims under the PUA program and claims under the Pandemic Emergency Unemployment Compensation (PEUC) program. Combining federal program claims with state program claims indicates there are a total of 17.7 million insured unemployed persons during the week ending January 30, 2021, compared to 14.4 million one month prior, during the week ending January 9, 2021.
