Over the week ending January 30, 2021, there were 348,900 initial UI \href{https://oui.doleta.gov/unemploy/DataDashboard.asp}{claims} under the Pandemic Unemployment Assistance (PUA) program (see {\color{blue!50!purple!80!black}\textbf{---}}), compared to 403,600 during the prior week, and an average of 358,400 initial claims per week over the past four weeks. 

Federal program continuing claims total 10,820,811 in January 23, 2021 (see {\color{green!50!blue}\textbf{---}}). These include both claims under the PUA program and claims under the Pandemic Emergency Unemployment Compensation (PEUC) program. Combining federal program claims with state program claims indicates there are a total of 15.9 million insured unemployed persons during the week ending January 23, 2021, compared to 17.4 million one month prior, during the week ending January 2, 2021.
