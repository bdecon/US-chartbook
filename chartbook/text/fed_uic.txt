Over the week ending May 22, 2021, there were 93,500 initial UI \href{https://oui.doleta.gov/unemploy/DataDashboard.asp}{claims} under the Pandemic Unemployment Assistance (PUA) program (see {\color{blue!50!purple!80!black}\textbf{---}}), compared to 95,100 during the prior week, and an average of 98,500 initial claims per week over the past four weeks. 

Federal program continuing claims total 11,707,299 in May 15, 2021 (see {\color{green!50!blue}\textbf{---}}). These include both claims under the PUA program and claims under the Pandemic Emergency Unemployment Compensation (PEUC) program. Combining federal program claims with state program claims indicates there are a total of 15.2 million insured unemployed persons during the week ending May 15, 2021, compared to 15.5 million one month prior, during the week ending April 24, 2021.
