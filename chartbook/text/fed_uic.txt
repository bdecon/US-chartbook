Over the week ending April 17, 2021, there were 133,300 initial UI \href{https://oui.doleta.gov/unemploy/DataDashboard.asp}{claims} under the Pandemic Unemployment Assistance (PUA) program (see {\color{blue!50!purple!80!black}\textbf{---}}), compared to 131,700 during the prior week, and an average of 164,400 initial claims per week over the past four weeks. 

Federal program continuing claims total 12,915,539 in April 10, 2021 (see {\color{green!50!blue}\textbf{---}}). These include both claims under the PUA program and claims under the Pandemic Emergency Unemployment Compensation (PEUC) program. Combining federal program claims with state program claims indicates there are a total of 16.8 million insured unemployed persons during the week ending April 10, 2021, compared to 17.0 million one month prior, during the week ending March 20, 2021.
