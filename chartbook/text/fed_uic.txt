Over the week ending April 10, 2021, there were 131,700 initial UI \href{https://oui.doleta.gov/unemploy/DataDashboard.asp}{claims} under the Pandemic Unemployment Assistance (PUA) program (see {\color{blue!50!purple!80!black}\textbf{---}}), compared to 152,200 during the prior week, and an average of 191,900 initial claims per week over the past four weeks. 

Federal program continuing claims total 12,201,366 in April 3, 2021 (see {\color{green!50!blue}\textbf{---}}). These include both claims under the PUA program and claims under the Pandemic Emergency Unemployment Compensation (PEUC) program. Combining federal program claims with state program claims indicates there are a total of 16.1 million insured unemployed persons during the week ending April 3, 2021, compared to 18.3 million one month prior, during the week ending March 13, 2021.
