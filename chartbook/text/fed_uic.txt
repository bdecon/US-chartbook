Over the week ending December 26, 2020, there were 308,300 initial UI \href{https://oui.doleta.gov/unemploy/DataDashboard.asp}{claims} under the Pandemic Unemployment Assistance (PUA) program (see {\color{blue!50!purple!80!black}\textbf{---}}), compared to 393,300 during the prior week, and an average of 389,700 initial claims per week over the past four weeks. 

Federal program continuing claims total 13,264,274 in December 19, 2020 (see {\color{green!50!blue}\textbf{---}}). These include both claims under the PUA program and claims under the Pandemic Emergency Unemployment Compensation (PEUC) program. Combining federal program claims with state program claims indicates there are a total of 18.6 million insured unemployed persons during the week ending December 19, 2020, compared to 18.6 million one month prior, during the week ending November 28, 2020.
