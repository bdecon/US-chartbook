In 2020 Q3, labor productivity increased at an annual rate of 4.9 percent (see\cbox{teal}), as the result of an increase of 43.5 percent in real ouput and an increase of 36.8 percent in hours worked. In the prior quarter, 2020 Q2, labor productivity increased at an annual rate of 10.6 percent, as real output decreased at an annual rate of 36.8 percent and hours of work decreased at an annual rate of 42.9 percent. Over the past five years, labor productivity growth has averaged 1.7 percent, slightly below the 1989-onward average of 2.0 percent.
