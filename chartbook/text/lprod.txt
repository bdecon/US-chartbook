In 2023 Q1, labor productivity decreased at an annual rate of 2.7 percent (see\cbox{teal}), as the result of an increase of 0.2 percent in real output and an increase of three percent in hours worked. In the prior quarter, 2022 Q4, labor productivity increased at an annual rate of 1.6 percent, as real output increased 2.9 percent and hours of work increased 1.3 percent. Over the past five years, labor productivity growth has averaged 1.4 percent, slightly below the 1989-onward average of 1.9 percent.
