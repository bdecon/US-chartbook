In 2022 Q4, labor productivity increased at an annual rate of three percent (see\cbox{teal}), as the result of an increase of 3.5 percent in real output and an increase of 0.5 percent in hours worked. In the prior quarter, 2022 Q3, labor productivity increased at an annual rate of 1.4 percent, as real output increased 3.6 percent and hours of work increased 2.3 percent. Over the past five years, labor productivity growth has averaged 1.8 percent, slightly below the 1989-onward average of 2.0 percent.
