In 2022 Q4, domestic holdings of foreign assets total \$31.7 trillion, equivalent to 121.2 percent of GDP (see {\color{blue!95!violet}\textbf{---}}). In 2022 Q3, these assets were equivalent to 115.6 percent of GDP, and in 2019, they were equivalent to 129.7 percent. Domestic liabilities to the foreign sector total \$47.8 trillion, or 182.9 percent of GDP, in 2022 Q4, following 181.0 percent in 2022 Q3, and 181.4 percent in 2019 (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}).

The overall result of these financial positions, net IIP, or holdings of foreign assets minus liabilities, identifies the US as a net debtor to the rest of the world, to the equivalent of 61.7 percent of GDP in 2022 Q4, following 65.4 percent in 2022 Q3, and 51.7 percent in 2019 (see \cbox{cyan!25!white}).
