In 2021 Q1, domestic holdings of foreign assets are equivalent to 148.7 percent of GDP (see {\color{blue!95!violet}\textbf{---}}), compared to 106.3 percent in 2006 Q1. Domestic liabilities to the foreign sector total 213.6 percent of GDP in 2021 Q1 (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}), and 118.5 percent in 2006 Q1. As a result, net IIP, holdings of foreign assets minus liabilities, identifies the US as a net debtor to the result of the world, to the equivalent of 64.9 percent of GDP in 2021 Q1 (see\cbox{cyan!25!white}) and 12.2 percent of GDP in 2006 Q1.
