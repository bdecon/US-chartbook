In August 2023, there were 22.7 million government jobs, equivalent to 6.8 for every 100 people (see {\color{blue!50!cyan}\textbf{---}}). The previous year, in August 2022, there were 22.2 million government jobs, equivalent to 6.7 per 100 people. During the 1990s, there were 7.3 government jobs per 100 people. If the rate was the same today, there would be 1.8 million additional government jobs.

By level of government, there were 14.5 million local government jobs in August 2023, equivalent to 4.3 per 100 people (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}). In the same period, there were 5.2 million state government job (1.6 per 100 people, see {\color{orange}\textbf{---}}), and 2.9 million federal government job (0.9 per 100 people, see {\color{green!80!blue}\textbf{---}}).

Since 2019, the US has lost 65,000 total government jobs. Of these, 148,000, or 227.7 percent of the shortfall, are local government jobs. During the same period, state governments lost 22,000 jobs, while the federal government added 105,000 jobs.
