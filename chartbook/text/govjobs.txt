In November 2023, there were 23.0 million government jobs, equivalent to 6.8 for every 100 people (see {\color{blue!50!cyan}\textbf{---}}). The previous year, in November 2022, there were 22.3 million government jobs, equivalent to 6.7 per 100 people. During the 1990s, there were 7.3 government jobs per 100 people. If the rate was the same today, there would be 1.5 million additional government jobs.

By level of government, there were 14.7 million local government jobs in November 2023, equivalent to 4.4 per 100 people (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}). In the same period, there were 5.3 million state government job (1.6 per 100 people, see {\color{orange}\textbf{---}}), and 3.0 million federal government job (0.9 per 100 people, see {\color{green!80!blue}\textbf{---}}).

Since 2019, the US has gained 244,000 total government jobs. During the same period, local governments added 41,000 jobs, state governments added 86,000 jobs, while the federal government added 117,000 jobs.
