Nonstore sales were 1.7 percent of after-tax income in January 1992 and 6.9 percent in April 2024, a shift that is equivalent to \$1,084 billion per year. Since 1992, sales as a share of after-tax income has decreased in food and beverage stores (-3.0 percentage points), clothing and accessories stores (-1.0 percentage points), and motor vehicles and parts stores (-1.0 percentage points).
