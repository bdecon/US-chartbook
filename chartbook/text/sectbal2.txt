Breaking out the two main categories in the private sector, households were net lenders (ran a surplus) of the equivalent of 1.7 percent of GDP in 2023 Q4 (see\cbox{orange!90!yellow}), while private businesses--corporate and noncorporate--were net lenders of the equivalent of 3.1 percent of GDP (see\cbox{purple!50!red}). In 2019, households were net lenders of 4.0 percent, and private businesses were net lenders of 0.6 percent.
