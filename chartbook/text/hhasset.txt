According to the US Financial Accounts produced by the Federal Reserve, the market value of \textbf{household and nonprofit assets} is \$176.2 trillion in 2023 Q4, equivalent to 630 percent--or 6.3 years--of GDP. Of this, \$57.9 trillion, or 32.9 percent of the total, are tangible (non-financial) assets and \$118.3 trillion, or 67.1 percent, are financial assets.

Tangible assets include peoples' homes as well as consumer durable goods, such as cars, furniture, and appliances. Owner-occupied real estate is valued at \$44.9 trillion in 2023 Q4, equivalent to 161 percent of GDP (see\cbox{green!60!teal}). The replacement value of consumer durable goods is \$7.9 trillion, or 28 percent of GDP.

Financial assets include equity in businesses--corporate and non-corporate--with a market value of \$62.7 trillion, or 224 percent of GDP (see\cbox{blue!65!black}), in 2023 Q4. Debt securities and loan assets total \$12.3 trillion, or 44 percent of GDP (see\cbox{blue!55!cyan}). Cash and deposits, including money market accounts, total \$18.1 trillion, or 65 percent of GDP (see\cbox{cyan!40!white}). Other financial assets total \$25.2 trillion.
