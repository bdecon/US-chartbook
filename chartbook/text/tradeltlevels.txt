US imports of goods and services totaled \$326.3 billion in August 2022, following imports of \$330.0 billion in July (see {\color{blue!80!violet}\textbf{---}}). Over the three months ending August 2022, imports averaged \$332.0 billion per month, compared to an average of \$284.8 billion per month one year prior, during the same three months. In 2019, imports averaged \$258.8 billion per month.

The US exported \$258.9 billion of goods and services in August 2022, following \$259.6 billion in July (see {\color{green!80!blue}\textbf{---}}). The three-month average was \$259.1 billion in August, and \$214.1 billion one year prior. Exports were \$212.2 billion per month, on average, in 2019.

Spending on imports exceeds payments received for exports, so the US runs a trade deficit. In August, the trade deficit was \$67.4 billion, following \$70.5 billion in July (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}). Over the past three months, the trade deficit averaged \$72.9 billion, compared to an average of \$70.7 billion one year prior, during the same three months. In 2019, the trade deficit averaged \$46.6 billion per month.
