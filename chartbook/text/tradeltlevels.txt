US goods and services imports total \$317.6 billion in December 2022, following \$313.4 billion in November (see {\color{blue!80!violet}\textbf{---}}). Imports average \$321.6 billion over the latest three months of data, and \$303.8 billion during the same months, one year prior. In 2019, monthly US imports average \$258.8 billion. For additional context, imports are equivalent to \$950 per capita, in the latest month.

The US exported \$250.2 billion of goods and services in December 2022, following \$252.3 billion in November (see {\color{green!80!blue}\textbf{---}}). The three-month average was \$253.0 billion in December, and \$228.8 billion one year prior. Exports were \$212.2 billion per month, on average, in 2019. In the latest month, exports are equivalent to \$748 per capita or \$1,571 per worker. 

Spending on imports exceeds payments received for exports, resulting in a trade deficit. In December, the trade deficit was \$67.4 billion, following \$61.0 billion in November (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}). Over the past three months, the average trade deficit is \$68.6 billion, compared to \$75.0 billion one year prior. In 2019, the average monthly trade deficit is \$46.6 billion.
