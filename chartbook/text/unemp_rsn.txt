There are several \textbf{reasons for unemployment}. In June 2024, 3.2 million people, or 1.9 percent of the labor force, were unemployed from losing their job (see\cbox{red!75!orange!70!white}). An additional 0.4 percent voluntarily left a job (see\cbox{blue!72!black}). Re-entrants, people who left the labor force but are looking for a new job, comprised 1.2 percent (see\cbox{blue!70!teal!70!gray!62!white}). Lastly, 0.4 percent of the labor force were new entrants to the labor market, looking for their first job (see\cbox{purple!80!red!85!black}).
