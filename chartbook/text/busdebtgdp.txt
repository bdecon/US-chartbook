As of 2021 Q2, nonfinancial business debt--the debt security and loan liabilities of nonfinancial businesses--both corporate and non-corporate--totals \$17,978 billion, with \$11,238 billion (62.5\%) held by corporate businesses. Over the past three years, nonfinancial business debt has increased faster than overall economic activity. As a share of GDP, nonfinancial business debt increased by 5.1 percentage points to 79.1 percent in 2021 Q2 from 74.0 percent in 2018 Q2. The vast majority of the increase, 1.7 percentage points, comes from nonbank loans (see\cbox{{blue}}).
