As of 2019 Q2, business debt--the debt security and loan liabilities of businesses--both corporate and non-corporate--totals \$15,764 billion, with \$9,973 billion (63.3\%) held by corporate businesses. Over the past three years, business debt has increased faster than than overall economic activity. As a share of GDP, business debt increased by 3.0 percentage points to 73.9 percent in 2019 Q2 from 70.9 percent in 2016 Q2. The vast majority of the increase, 2.6 percentage points, comes from nonbank loans (see\cbox{blue}).
