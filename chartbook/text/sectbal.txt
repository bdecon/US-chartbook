In 2021 Q1, the US private sector was a net lender (running a surplus) of the equivalent of 17.2 percent of GDP, far above the 2.6 percent surplus in 2015 Q1. The rest of the world was a net lender to the US to the equivalent of 3.9 percent of GDP in 2021 Q1, compared to 2.4 percent in 2015 Q1. Balancing these transactions, the government (federal, state, and local combined) was a net borrower (running a deficit) of the equivalent of 21.1 percent of GDP, compared to 5.0 percent in 2015.
