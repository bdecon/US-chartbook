In 2023 Q4, the US private sector was a net lender (running a surplus) of the equivalent of 4.7 percent of GDP, in line with the 4.6 percent surplus in 2019. The rest of the world was a net lender to the US to the equivalent of 2.9 percent of GDP in 2023 Q4, compared to 2.1 percent in 2019. Balancing these transactions, the government (federal, state, and local combined) was a net borrower (running a deficit) of the equivalent of 7.6 percent of GDP in 2023 Q4, compared to 6.7 percent in 2019.
