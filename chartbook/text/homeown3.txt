During the housing bubble, the homeownership rate for black households increased by nearly ten percentage points. Black homeownership peaked at 49.7 percent in the second quarter of 2004 but fell to 40.6 percent in 2019 Q2. The 2022 Q2 rate is 45.3 percent (see {\color{red!20!orange!95!black}\textbf{---}}). The 2022 Q2 rate for Hispanic households is 48.3 percent, substantially below the 50.1 percent rate in 2007 Q1 (see {\color{green!80!blue!85!black}\textbf{---}}).
