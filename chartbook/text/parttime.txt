In March 2023, 4.1 million people worked part-time for economic reasons, equivalent to 2.5 percent of the labor force (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}). In 2019, an average of 2.7 percent of the labor force worked part-time for economic reasons; in 2010, following the great recession, the rate was 5.8 percent.

Voluntary part-time workers total 21.4 million in March 2023, or 12.9 percent of the labor force (see {\color{orange!80!yellow}\textbf{---}}). The category made up 13.1 percent of the labor force in 2019, on average.
