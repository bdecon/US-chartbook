In May 2024, 4.4 million people worked part-time for economic reasons, equivalent to 2.6 percent of the labor force (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}). In 2019, an average of 2.7 percent of the labor force worked part-time for economic reasons. In 2010, following the great recession, the rate was 5.8 percent.

Voluntary part-time workers total 22.7 million in May 2024, or 13.5 percent of the labor force (see {\color{orange!80!yellow}\textbf{---}}). The category is 13.1 percent of the labor force in 2019, on average.
