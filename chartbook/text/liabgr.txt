Domestic liabilities increased by 4.4 percent over the year ending 2019 Q4, after adjusting for inflation. Over the past three years, total domestic liabilities increased at an average annual rate of 3.0 percent. The federal government contributed 0.8 percentage point per year on average (see\cbox{blue!70}), while the state and local government subtracted 0.1 percentage point per year on average (see\cbox{cyan!52}). Households and nonprofits contributed an annual average of 0.3 percentage point over this three year period (see\cbox{orange!70}), corporate businesses contributed 1.5 percentage points per year on average (see\cbox{lime!70}), and non-corporate businesses contributed 0.5 percentage point per year on average (see\cbox{green!72!black}).
