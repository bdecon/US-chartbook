The core CPI, which excludes food and energy, increased 0.2 percent in June 2023, or 1.9 percent annualized, far below the one-year core CPI inflation rate of 4.9 percent. The core CPI increased 0.4 percent in both May 2023 and in April 2023.

In June, housing prices increased 0.3 percent, (3.7 percent annualized). Over the past three months, housing prices increased at an average annualized rate of 3.2 percent, substantially below the 12-month rate of 4.9 percent. Food prices increased 0.1 percent in June, or 1.3 percent, annualized, compared to a three-month average of 1.3 percent.

Transportation prices increased at an annualized rate of 1.8 percent in June, and increased at an average annualized rate of 4.8 percent over the past three months. Energy prices increased at an annualized rate of 7.5 percent in June, and decreased at an average annualized rate of seven percent over the past three months.
