The core CPI, which excludes food and energy, increased 0.4 percent in April 2023, or 5.0 percent annualized, substantially below the one-year core CPI inflation rate of 5.5 percent. The core CPI increased 0.4 percent in March 2023, and increased 0.5 percent in February 2023.

In April, housing prices increased 0.2 percent, (2.9 percent annualized). Over the past three months, housing prices increased at an average annualized rate of 4.4 percent, substantially below the 12-month rate of 5.5 percent. Food prices was virtually unchanged in April, or 0.3 percent, annualized, compared to a three-month average of 1.7 percent.

Transportation prices increased at an annualized rate of 14.8 percent in April, and increased at an average annualized rate of 3.5 percent over the past three months. Energy prices increased at an annualized rate of seven percent in April, and decreased at an average annualized rate of 11.5 percent over the past three months.
