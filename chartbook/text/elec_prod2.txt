During the 12 months ending December 2021, the US generated 4,116 billion kilowatt hours of electricity. Of this, 1,575 billion kilowatt hours were generated using natural gas (see\cbox{blue!40!cyan!60!white}), 899 billion kilowatt hours were generated from coal (see\cbox{brown}), 778 billion from nuclear (see\cbox{{red!80!blue!70!white}}), and 826 billion from renewable sources (see\cbox{{green!75!blue}}).
