Real per capita consumer spending increased at an average rate of 6.9 percent over the four quarters ending 2021 Q4. Changes to disposable income subtracted 0.1 percentage point, changes to saving added seven percentage points, and changes to other outlays didn't affect the total. During 2019, real per capita consumer spending increased at an average rate of 1.7 percent. Increased income contributed two percentage points, and a slight increase in saving subtracted 0.2 percentage point.
