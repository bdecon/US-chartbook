Real per capita consumer spending decreased at an average rate of 9.6 percent over the four quarters ending 2020 Q2. Changes to disposable income added 11.2 percentage points, changes to saving subtracted 21.3 percentage points, and changes to other outlays added 0.5 percentage points. Over the past three years, real per capita consumer spending growth has averaged -1.8 percent, with income growth contribuing an average of 5.4 percentage points and saving subtracting an average of 7.2 percentage points.
