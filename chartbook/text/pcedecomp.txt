Real per capita consumer spending increased at an average rate of 6.7 percent over the four quarters ending 2021 Q3. Changes to disposable income subtracted 2.1 percentage points, changes to saving added 8.7 percentage points, and changes to other outlays added 0.1 percentage points. Over the past three years, real per capita consumer spending growth has averaged 2.9 percent, with income growth contribuing an average of 1.2 percentage points and saving subtracting an average of 1.5 percentage points.
