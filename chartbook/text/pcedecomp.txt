Real per capita consumer spending was unchanged over the four quarters ending 2020 Q1. Changes to disposable income added 1.1 percentage points, changes to saving subtracted 1.2 percentage points, and changes to other outlays added 0.1 percentage points. Over the past three years, real per capita consumer spending growth has averaged 1.4 percent, with income growth contribuing an average of 2.6 percentage points and saving subtracting an average of 1.1 percentage points.
