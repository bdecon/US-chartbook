By level of government, federal current expenditures are equivalent to 20.1 percent of GDP in 2024 Q2, and 19.9 percent in 2023 Q2 (see\cbox{blue!65!gray!90!white}). Federal government transfers to state and local governments comprise 3.3 percent of GDP in the latest data (see\cbox{cyan!40!white}). State and local government current expenditures, excluding transfers from the federal government, are equivalent to 10.5 percent of GDP in 2024 Q2 (see\cbox{red!20!purple!70!white}). 

By category, consumption expenditures represent 13.7 percent of GDP in 2024 Q2, and 13.7 percent of GDP one year prior. Over the past 30 years, consumption expenditures average 14.8 percent of GDP. Current transfer payments, which are largely government social benefits, total 15.0 percent of GDP in 2024 Q2, compared to 15.3 percent one year prior, and a long-term average of 13.6 percent.

Consolidated government interest payments are 4.8 percent of GDP in the latest data, compared to 4.4 percent one year prior. Interest payments comprise 4.4 percent over the past 30 years, on average. Government subsidies to businesses total 0.3 percent of GDP in 2024 Q2 and 0.4 percent in 2023 Q2.
