As of October 2022, the \textbf{household} population is 323.9 million, or 97.7 percent of the total resident population. The \textbf{group quarters} population is \href{https://www.census.gov/library/stories/2021/08/united-states-group-quarters-in-2020-census.html}{measured} in depth as part of the 2020 Census. The 2020 group quarters population is 8.2 million, of which 3.8 million are institutionalized. Of these, two million are in prisons and jails, and 1.6 million are in nursing and skilled-care facilities. An additional 2.8 million people live in dormitories or student housing, 328,000 live in barracks, and 1.4 million live in other noninstitutional facilities such as shelters and group homes.
