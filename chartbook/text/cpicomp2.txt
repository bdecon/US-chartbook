Housing prices increased two percent over the year ending December 2020, slightly below the pre-COVID rate of 2.7 percent (covering the year ending February 2020). Medical care prices increased 1.8 percent, these prices grew 4.6 percent over the year ending February 2020. In contrast, prices of food consumed at home (groceries) increased 3.9 percent in the year ending December 2020 compared to 0.8 percent over the year ending February 2020. 

Transportation prices decreased 2.4 percent over the year ending December 2020, far below the pre-COVID 1.7 percent increase. Energy prices decreased seven percent in the latest month, compared to a 2.8 percent increase in February 2020. Energy prices are historically more volatile than other categories.
