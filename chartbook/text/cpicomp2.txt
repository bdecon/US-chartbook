Housing prices increased 1.8 percent over the year ending February 2021, slightly below the pre-COVID rate of 2.7 percent (covering the year ending February 2020). Medical care prices increased two percent, these prices grew 4.6 percent over the year ending February 2020. In contrast, prices of food consumed at home (groceries) increased 3.5 percent in the year ending February 2021 compared to 0.8 percent over the year ending February 2020. 

Transportation prices increased 0.6 percent over the year ending February 2021, substantially below the pre-COVID 1.7 percent increase. Energy prices increased 2.4 percent in the latest month, compared to a 2.8 percent increase in February 2020. Energy prices are historically more volatile than other categories.
