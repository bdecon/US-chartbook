Housing prices increased 3.5 percent over the year ending August 2021, slightly above the pre-COVID rate of 2.7 percent (covering the year ending February 2020). Medical care prices increased 0.4 percent, these prices grew 4.6 percent over the year ending February 2020. In contrast, prices of food consumed at home (groceries) increased three percent in the year ending August 2021 compared to 0.8 percent over the year ending February 2020. 

Transportation prices increased 17.8 percent over the year ending August 2021, far above the pre-COVID 1.7 percent increase. Energy prices increased 25.0 percent in the latest month, compared to a 2.8 percent increase in February 2020. Energy prices are historically more volatile than other categories.
