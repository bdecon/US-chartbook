Housing prices increased 5.1 percent over the year ending December 2021, substantially above the pre-COVID rate of 2.7 percent (covering the year ending February 2020). Medical care prices increased 2.2 percent, these prices grew 4.6 percent over the year ending February 2020. In contrast, prices of food consumed at home (groceries) increased 6.5 percent in the year ending December 2021 compared to 0.8 percent over the year ending February 2020. 

Transportation prices increased 21.1 percent over the year ending December 2021, far above the pre-COVID 1.7 percent increase. Energy prices increased 29.3 percent in the latest month, compared to a 2.8 percent increase in February 2020. Energy prices are historically more volatile than other categories.
