Housing prices increased 5.7 percent over the year ending January 2022, substantially above the pre-COVID rate of 2.7 percent (covering the year ending February 2020). Medical care prices increased 2.5 percent, these prices grew 4.6 percent over the year ending February 2020. In contrast, prices of food consumed at home (groceries) increased 7.4 percent in the year ending January 2022 compared to 0.8 percent over the year ending February 2020. 

Transportation prices increased 20.8 percent over the year ending January 2022, far above the pre-COVID 1.7 percent increase. Energy prices increased 27.0 percent in the latest month, compared to a 2.8 percent increase in February 2020. Energy prices are historically more volatile than other categories.
