As of the fourth quarter of 2022, the modified Taylor rule suggests a federal funds rate of 4.7 percent, 0.08 percentage point below the current rate.

FOMC meeting participants provide \href{https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomccalendars.htm}{projections} which can be used to summarize policymaker views on the future path of the federal funds rate, as seen by the people who set it. As of March 24, 2023, the median projected federal funds rate rate is 5.1 percent for 2023, 4.3 percent for 2024, and 3.1 percent for 2025 (see \cbox{purple}).
