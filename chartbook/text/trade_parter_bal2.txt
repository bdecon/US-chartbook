The US also runs a trade deficit with the European Union. In 1997, trade between the EU and US was relatively balanced. In the latest data, the goods trade deficit with the EU is 0.8 percent of GDP.

 The US trade deficit with Mexico is currently 0.5 percent of GDP. In the early 1990s, the US had a trade surplus with Mexico. The US has a surplus with south and central American countries, equivalent to 0.2 percent of GDP.
