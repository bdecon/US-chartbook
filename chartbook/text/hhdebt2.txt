The ratio of household and nonprofit debt to disposable personal income has fallen to 101.3 percent in 2022 Q4 from its housing-bubble peak of 136.6 percent in 2007 Q4. Over the past three years, household and nonprofit debt has increased 17.5 percent while disposable personal income has increased 15.1 percent. As a result, the ratio of household and nonprofit debt to disposable personal income has increased by two percentage points.
