The ratio of household and nonprofit debt to disposable personal income has fallen to 88.2 percent in 2021 Q1 from its housing-bubble peak of 135.6 percent in 2007 Q4. Over the past three years, nominal household and nonprofit debt has increased 11.5 percent while nominal disposable personal income has increased 16.6 percent. As a result, the ratio of household and nonprofit debt to disposable personal income has fallen by 12.6 percentage points.
