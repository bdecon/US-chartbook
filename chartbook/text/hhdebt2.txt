The ratio of household and nonprofit debt to disposable personal income has fallen to 90.0 percent in 2020 Q2 from its housing-bubble peak of 136.0 percent in 2007 Q4. Over the past three years, nominal household and nonprofit debt has increased 9.5 percent while nominal disposable personal income has increased 17.9 percent. As a result, the ratio of household and nonprofit debt to disposable personal income has fallen by 11.5 percentage points.
