The ratio of household and nonprofit debt to disposable personal income has fallen to 100.3 percent in 2023 Q4 from the housing bubble peak of 137.3 percent in 2007. Over the past four years, household and nonprofit debt has increased 23.5 percent while disposable personal income increased 15.6 percent. As a result, the debt-to-income ratio has fallen by 1.2 percentage points.
