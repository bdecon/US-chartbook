The ratio of household and nonprofit debt to disposable personal income has fallen to 97.9 percent in 2021 Q2 from its housing-bubble peak of 136.6 percent in 2007 Q4. Over the past three years, nominal household and nonprofit debt has increased 12.5 percent while nominal disposable personal income has increased 14.6 percent. As a result, the ratio of household and nonprofit debt to disposable personal income has fallen by 3.2 percentage points.
