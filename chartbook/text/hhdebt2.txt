The ratio of household and nonprofit debt to disposable personal income has fallen to 103.2 percent in 2022 Q3 from its housing-bubble peak of 136.6 percent in 2007 Q4. Over the past three years, household and nonprofit debt has increased 17.8 percent while disposable personal income has increased 13.9 percent. As a result, the ratio of household and nonprofit debt to disposable personal income has increased by four percentage points.
