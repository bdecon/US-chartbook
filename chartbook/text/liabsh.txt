Private business liabilities, corporate- and non-corporate, total \$34.4 trillion in the second quarter of 2022, equivalent to 136.4 percent of GDP (see {\color{green!72!black}\textbf{---}}). In 2019, business liabilities were equivalent to 143.5 percent of GDP, and during the 1990s they were 99.6 percent of GDP, on average. Private household and nonprofit liabilities are equivalent to 74.9 percent of GDP in 2022 Q2 (see {\color{orange!90!red}\textbf{---}}), 76.0 percent in 2019, and 64.2 percent in the 1990s.

Federal government liabilities are equivalent to 114.4 percent of GDP in 2022 Q2 (see {\color{blue!70!white}\textbf{---}}), compared to 98.5 percent of GDP in 2019 and 66.2 percent of GDP during the 1990s. Combined state and local government liabilities total 35.3 percent of GDP in 2022 Q2 (see {\color{cyan!60!white}\textbf{---}}), 39.3 percent in 2019, and 26.8 percent in the 1990s.
