In June 2024, all 50 states and DC had unemployment rates below eight percent, and the unemployment rate was above five percent in five states. Three months prior, in March 2024, no states unemployment rate was above eight percent, and three states were above five percent. In the peak of the COVID-19 pandemic shutdowns, in April 2020, the unemployment rate was above eight percent in 48 states, and above five percent in every state.

The states with the highest unemployment rates in June 2024 are Illinois (6.1\%), the District of Columbia (6.1\%), and Nevada (6.0\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Vermont (2.1\%), South Dakota (2.1\%), and New Hampshire (2.4\%).
