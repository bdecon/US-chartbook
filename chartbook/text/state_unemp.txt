In July 2021, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 41 states in April 2020. In July 2020, 15 states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only 50 states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In July 2021, 22 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in July 2021 are Nevada (8.2\%), California (7.9\%), and New Mexico (7.7\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Nebraska (2.1\%), Utah (2.7\%), and South Dakota (2.7\%).
