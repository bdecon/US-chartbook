In February 2021, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 41 states in April 2020. In February 2020, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only four states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In February 2021, 33 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in February 2021 are New York (9.6\%), Hawaii (8.5\%), and Connecticut (8.5\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Vermont (3.3\%), New Hampshire (3.3\%), and South Dakota (3.4\%).
