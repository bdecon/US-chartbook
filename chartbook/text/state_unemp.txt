In December 2022, all 50 states and DC had unemployment rates below eight percent, and the unemployment rate was above five percent in one state. Three months prior, in September 2022, no states unemployment rate was above eight percent, and one state was above five percent. In the peak of the COVID-19 pandemic shutdowns, in April 2020, the unemployment rate was above eight percent in 48 states, and above five percent in every state.

The states with the highest unemployment rates in December 2022 are Nevada (5.1\%), Washington (4.6\%), and Oregon (4.6\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are North Dakota (2.0\%), Utah (2.1\%), and Alabama (2.2\%).
