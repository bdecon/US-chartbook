In May 2021, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 41 states in April 2020. In May 2020, 30 states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only 51 states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In May 2021, 23 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in May 2021 are Nevada (7.7\%), Hawaii (7.5\%), and California (7.5\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Vermont (1.4\%), New Hampshire (1.4\%), and Nebraska (2.3\%).
