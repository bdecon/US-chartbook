In October 2023, all 50 states and DC had unemployment rates below eight percent, and the unemployment rate was above five percent in two states. Three months prior, in July 2023, no states unemployment rate was above eight percent, and two states were above five percent. In the peak of the COVID-19 pandemic shutdowns, in April 2020, the unemployment rate was above eight percent in 48 states, and above five percent in every state.

The states with the highest unemployment rates in October 2023 are the District of Columbia (5.2\%), Nevada (5.1\%), and California (4.8\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are North Dakota (1.4\%), Vermont (1.8\%), and South Dakota (2.1\%).
