In March 2021, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 41 states in April 2020. In March 2020, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only 15 states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In March 2021, 29 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in March 2021 are Hawaii (8.6\%), New York (8.5\%), and Connecticut (8.3\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Nebraska (2.8\%), Utah (2.9\%), and New Hampshire (3.2\%).
