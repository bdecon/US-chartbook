In April 2024, all 50 states and DC had unemployment rates below eight percent, and the unemployment rate was above five percent in no states. Three months prior, in January 2024, no states unemployment rate was above eight percent, and seven states were above five percent. In the peak of the COVID-19 pandemic shutdowns, in April 2020, the unemployment rate was above eight percent in 48 states, and above five percent in every state.

The states with the highest unemployment rates in April 2024 are Nevada (5.0\%), California (4.8\%), and the District of Columbia (4.6\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Mississippi (1.8\%), South Dakota (2.0\%), and Iowa (2.2\%).
