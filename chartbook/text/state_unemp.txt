In August 2022, all 50 states and DC had unemployment rates below eight percent, and the unemployment rate was above five percent in two states. Three months prior, in May 2022, no states unemployment rate was above eight percent, and no states were above five percent. In the peak of the COVID-19 pandemic shutdowns, in April 2020, the unemployment rate was above eight percent in 49 states, and above five percent in every state.

The states with the highest unemployment rates in August 2022 are the District of Columbia (5.3\%), Nevada (5.2\%), and New York (4.9\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Vermont (2.0\%), Utah (2.1\%), and Minnesota (2.1\%).
