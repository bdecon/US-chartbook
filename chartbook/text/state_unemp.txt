In December 2020, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 41 states in April 2020. In December 2019, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only two states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In December 2020, 33 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in December 2020 are Hawaii (9.3\%), California (9.1\%), and District of Columbia (8.8\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Utah (3.0\%), Nebraska (3.0\%), and Vermont (3.3\%).
