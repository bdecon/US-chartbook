In January 2021, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 41 states in April 2020. In January 2020, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only six states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In January 2021, 38 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in January 2021 are Hawaii (9.8\%), New York (9.4\%), and California (9.2\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Nebraska (3.1\%), South Dakota (3.3\%), and Utah (3.4\%).
