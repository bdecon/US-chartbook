In September 2020, four states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 44 states in April 2020. In September 2019, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only three states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In September 2020, 41 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in September 2020 are Hawaii (15.3\%), Nevada (12.6\%), and California (10.8\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Nebraska (3.3\%), South Dakota (3.8\%), and North Dakota (3.8\%).
