In November 2020, one states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 44 states in April 2020. In November 2019, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only two states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In November 2020, 31 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in November 2020 are Hawaii (10.1\%), Nevada (9.9\%), and New Jersey (9.9\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Nebraska (2.7\%), Vermont (2.8\%), and Iowa (3.1\%).
