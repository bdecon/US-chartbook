In August 2020, 10 states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 44 states in April 2020. In August 2019, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only four states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In August 2020, 45 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in August 2020 are Nevada (13.3\%), Rhode Island (13.0\%), and New York (12.6\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Idaho (3.8\%), Nebraska (3.9\%), and Utah (4.2\%).
