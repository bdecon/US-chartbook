In September 2021, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 41 states in April 2020. In September 2020, five states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only 41 states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In September 2021, 13 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in September 2021 are California (6.4\%), Nevada (6.4\%), and Hawaii (6.4\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Nebraska (1.4\%), Utah (1.6\%), and Montana (1.9\%).
