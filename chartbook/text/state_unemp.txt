In February 2023, all 50 states and DC had unemployment rates below eight percent, and the unemployment rate was above five percent in one state. Three months prior, in November 2022, no states unemployment rate was above eight percent, and one state was above five percent. In the peak of the COVID-19 pandemic shutdowns, in April 2020, the unemployment rate was above eight percent in 48 states, and above five percent in every state.

The states with the highest unemployment rates in February 2023 are Nevada (5.7\%), Washington (5.0\%), and the District of Columbia (4.9\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Nebraska (2.2\%), Alabama (2.4\%), and New Hampshire (2.4\%).
