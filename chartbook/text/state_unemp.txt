In December 2021, no states had an unemployment rate above eight percent, compared to 49 states in April 2020. One year prior, in December 2020, four states had an unemployment rate above eight percent and 30 states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In December 2021, three states have an unemployment rate above five percent.

The states with the highest unemployment rates in December 2021 are Alaska (5.2\%), New Mexico (5.2\%), and District of Columbia (5.1\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Indiana (1.5\%), Nebraska (1.8\%), and Utah (1.8\%).
