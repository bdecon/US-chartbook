In April 2021, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 41 states in April 2020. In April 2020, 41 states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only 51 states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In April 2021, 26 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in April 2021 are Hawaii (8.1\%), California (8.1\%), and Nevada (7.9\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Nebraska (2.4\%), New Hampshire (2.7\%), and Utah (2.8\%).
