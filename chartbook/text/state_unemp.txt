In August 2021, no states had an unemployment rate above ten percent, compared to 41 states in April 2020. In August 2020, eight states had an unemployment rate above ten percent and only 44 states had an unemployment rate above five percent. In August 2021, 21 states have an unemployment rate above five percent. 

The states with the highest unemployment rates in August 2021 are California (7.5\%), Nevada (7.1\%), and New York (7.1\%). The states with the lowest unemployment rates are Nebraska (1.8\%), Utah (2.5\%), and Idaho (2.6\%).
