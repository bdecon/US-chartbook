One measure of real interest rates comes from Treasury inflation-indexed securities. The yield on these securities can be a proxy for the interest rate investors would charge for treasuries, without inflation. 

As of October 24, 2023, the real yield on ten-year treasuries is 2.43 percent (see {\color{green!85!blue}\textbf{---}}), compared to 1.49 percent three months prior, on July 21. Five-year treasuries yield 2.44 percent in the latest data, and 1.86 percent three months prior, after adjusting for expected inflation (see {\color{violet!50!blue!60!white}\textbf{---}}).
