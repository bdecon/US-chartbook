Real per capita consumer spending over the past three months compared with the previous three months shows annualized growth of 1.7 percent in April 2024. Higher income contributed 0.6 percentage point, reduced saving contributed 0.7 percentage point, and decreases in other outlays contributed 0.4 percentage point.

 Higher interest rates, which count as other outlays, can eat into consumer spending. Since the start of 2022, increases in other outlays have reduced consumer spending by 0.5 percentage point per year.
