The Bureau of Labor Statistics \href{https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ximpim.nr0.htm}{report} changes in the prices of imports and exports. Over the year ending January 2022, US import prices grew 10.8 percent (see {\color{cyan!85!yellow}\textbf{---}}), following an increase of 10.2 percent in December 2021 and 11.8 percent in November. Excluding fuels, US import prices increased 6.9 percent in January 2022 and grew 6.4 percent in December 2021. Over the three years ending February 2020, prior to the US COVID-19 pandemic, US import prices increased at an average rate of 1.3 percent. Excluding fuels, import prices increased at an average rate of 0.3 percent during the same three-year pre-COVID period.

Prices of US exports (see {\color{red!25!orange}\textbf{---}}) grew 15.1 percent over the year ending January 2022, compared to 14.8 percent in December 2021, 18.1 percent in November, and 1.5 percent on average during the three years ending February 2020.
