The Bureau of Labor Statistics \href{https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ximpim.nr0.htm}{report} changes in the prices of imports and exports. Over the year ending December 2020, US import prices fell 0.3 percent (see {\color{cyan!85!yellow}\textbf{---}}), following a decrease of one percent in November and 1.0 percent in October. Excluding fuels, US import prices increased 1.9 percent in December 2020 and grew 1.6 percent in November. Over the three years ending February 2020, prior to the US COVID-19 pandemic, US import prices increased at an average rate of 1.3 percent. Excluding fuels, import prices increased at an average rate of 0.3 percent during the same three-year pre-COVID period.

Prices of US exports (see {\color{red!25!orange}\textbf{---}}) grew 0.2 percent over the year ending December 2020, compared to a decrease of 1.1 percent in November, 1.7 percent in October, and an increase of 1.5 percent on average during the three years ending February 2020.
