The Bureau of Labor Statistics \href{https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ximpim.nr0.htm}{report} changes in the prices of imports and exports. Over the year ending September 2020, US import prices fell 1.1 percent (see {\color{cyan!85!yellow}\textbf{---}}), following a decrease of 1.4 percent in August and 2.9 percent in July. Excluding fuels, US import prices increased 1.5 percent in September 2020 and grew 0.8 percent in August. Over the three years ending February 2020, prior to the US COVID-19 pandemic, US import prices increased at an average rate of 1.3 percent. Excluding fuels, import prices increased at an average rate of 0.3 percent during the same three-year pre-COVID period.

Prices of US exports (see {\color{red!25!orange}\textbf{---}}) fell 1.8 percent over the year ending September 2020, compared to 2.7 percent in August, 3.7 percent in July, and an increase of 1.5 percent on average during the three years ending February 2020.
