The Bureau of Labor Statistics \href{https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ximpim.nr0.htm}{report} changes in the prices of imports and exports. Over the year ending October 2021, US import prices grew 10.7 percent (see {\color{cyan!85!yellow}\textbf{---}}), following an increase of 9.3 percent in September and 9.0 percent in August. Excluding fuels, US import prices increased 5.5 percent in October 2021 and grew 5.1 percent in September. Over the three years ending February 2020, prior to the US COVID-19 pandemic, US import prices increased at an average rate of 1.3 percent. Excluding fuels, import prices increased at an average rate of 0.3 percent during the same three-year pre-COVID period.

Prices of US exports (see {\color{red!25!orange}\textbf{---}}) grew 18.0 percent over the year ending October 2021, compared to 16.5 percent in September, 16.7 percent in August, and 1.5 percent on average during the three years ending February 2020.
