The Bureau of Labor Statistics \href{https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ximpim.nr0.htm}{report} changes in the prices of imports and exports. Over the year ending November 2020, US import prices fell one percent (see {\color{cyan!85!yellow}\textbf{---}}), following a decrease of one percent in October and 1.3 percent in September. Excluding fuels, US import prices increased 1.6 percent in November 2020 and grew 1.7 percent in October. Over the three years ending February 2020, prior to the US COVID-19 pandemic, US import prices increased at an average rate of 1.3 percent. Excluding fuels, import prices increased at an average rate of 0.3 percent during the same three-year pre-COVID period.

Prices of US exports (see {\color{red!25!orange}\textbf{---}}) fell 1.1 percent over the year ending November 2020, compared to 1.6 percent in October, 1.8 percent in September, and an increase of 1.5 percent on average during the three years ending February 2020.
