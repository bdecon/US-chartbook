The Bureau of Labor Statistics \href{https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ximpim.nr0.htm}{report} changes in the prices of imports and exports. Over the year ending April 2023, \textbf{US import prices} fell 4.8 percent (see {\color{cyan!85!yellow}\textbf{---}}), following a decrease of 4.8 percent in March and 1.2 percent in February. Excluding fuels, US import prices decreased 1.9 percent in April 2023 and fell 1.5 percent in March. Over the three years ending February 2020, prior to the US COVID-19 pandemic, US import prices increased at an average rate of 1.3 percent. Excluding fuels, import prices increased at an average rate of 0.3 percent during the same three-year pre-COVID period.

\textbf{Prices of US exports} (see {\color{red!25!orange}\textbf{---}}) fell 5.9 percent over the year ending April 2023, compared to 5.2 percent in March, 0.8 percent in February, and an increase of 1.5 percent on average during the three years ending February 2020.
