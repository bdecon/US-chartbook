The Bureau of Labor Statistics \href{https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ximpim.nr0.htm}{report} changes in the prices of imports and exports. Over the year ending July 2023, \textbf{US import prices} fell 4.4 percent (see {\color{cyan!85!yellow}\textbf{---}}), following a decrease of 6.1 percent in June and 5.7 percent in May. Excluding fuels, US import prices decreased 0.9 percent in July 2023 and fell 1.3 percent in June. Over the three years ending February 2020, prior to the US COVID-19 pandemic, US import prices increased at an average rate of 1.3 percent. Excluding fuels, import prices increased at an average rate of 0.3 percent during the same three-year pre-COVID period.

\textbf{Prices of US exports} (see {\color{red!25!orange}\textbf{---}}) fell 7.9 percent over the year ending July 2023, compared to 11.9 percent in June, 10.3 percent in May, and an increase of 1.5 percent on average during the three years ending February 2020.
