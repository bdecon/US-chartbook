The Bureau of Labor Statistics \href{https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ximpim.nr0.htm}{report} changes in the prices of imports and exports. Over the year ending June 2023, \textbf{US import prices} fell 6.1 percent (see {\color{cyan!85!yellow}\textbf{---}}), following a decrease of 5.7 percent in May and 4.9 percent in April. Excluding fuels, US import prices decreased 1.4 percent in June 2023 and fell 1.6 percent in May. Over the three years ending February 2020, prior to the US COVID-19 pandemic, US import prices increased at an average rate of 1.3 percent. Excluding fuels, import prices increased at an average rate of 0.3 percent during the same three-year pre-COVID period.

\textbf{Prices of US exports} (see {\color{red!25!orange}\textbf{---}}) fell 12.0 percent over the year ending June 2023, compared to 10.2 percent in May, 6.0 percent in April, and an increase of 1.5 percent on average during the three years ending February 2020.
