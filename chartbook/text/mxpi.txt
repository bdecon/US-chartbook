The Bureau of Labor Statistics \href{https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ximpim.nr0.htm}{report} changes in the prices of imports and exports. Over the year ending August 2021, US import prices grew nine percent (see {\color{cyan!85!yellow}\textbf{---}}), following an increase of 10.3 percent in July and 11.3 percent in June. Excluding fuels, US import prices increased 5.6 percent in August 2021 and grew 6.4 percent in July. Over the three years ending February 2020, prior to the US COVID-19 pandemic, US import prices increased at an average rate of 1.3 percent. Excluding fuels, import prices increased at an average rate of 0.3 percent during the same three-year pre-COVID period.

Prices of US exports (see {\color{red!25!orange}\textbf{---}}) grew 16.8 percent over the year ending August 2021, compared to 17.0 percent in July, 16.9 percent in June, and 1.5 percent on average during the three years ending February 2020.
