The Bureau of Labor Statistics \href{https://www.bls.gov/news.release/ximpim.nr0.htm}{report} changes in the prices of imports and exports. Over the year ending January 2023, \textbf{US import prices} grew 0.8 percent (see {\color{cyan!85!yellow}\textbf{---}}), following an increase of three percent in December 2022 and 2.7 percent in November. Excluding fuels, US import prices increased 0.8 percent in January 2023 and grew 1.9 percent in December 2022. Over the three years ending February 2020, prior to the US COVID-19 pandemic, US import prices increased at an average rate of 1.3 percent. Excluding fuels, import prices increased at an average rate of 0.3 percent during the same three-year pre-COVID period.

\textbf{Prices of US exports} (see {\color{red!25!orange}\textbf{---}}) grew 2.3 percent over the year ending January 2023, compared to 4.3 percent in December 2022, 6.1 percent in November, and 1.5 percent on average during the three years ending February 2020.
