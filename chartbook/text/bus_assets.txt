Combined \textbf{assets of private nonfinancial businesses} are valued at \$87.2 trillion in the third quarter of 2023, which is 315.7 percent of GDP. These include financial and nonfinancial assets. Financial assets include cash and deposits, equity in other businesses, trade receivables, and other financial assets, and total \$36.6 trillion. Nonfinancial, or tangible, assets include real estate, equipment, inventories, and intellectual property products, and total \$50.6 trillion.

Nonfinancial corporations have assets valued at \$59.6 trillion, or 215.8 percent of GDP. These include nonfinancial assets (see\cbox{green!52!blue!55!white}) valued at 112.9 percent of GDP and financial assets (see\cbox{green!70!yellow!70!lightgray!90!white}) valued at 102.9 percent, as of 2023 Q3. Noncorporate business assets are valued at \$27.6 trillion, equivalent to 99.9 percent of GDP. Tangible assets (see\cbox{orange!50!red!45!white}) are equivalent to 70.3 percent of GDP, and include \$10.4 trillion in rental housing. Financial assets for the sector (see\cbox{orange!55!red}) total 29.6 percent of GDP.
