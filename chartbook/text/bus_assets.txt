Combined \textbf{assets of private nonfinancial businesses} are valued at \$87.7 trillion in the fourth quarter of 2023, which is 313.5 percent of GDP. These include financial and nonfinancial assets. Financial assets include cash and deposits, equity in other businesses, trade receivables, and other financial assets, and total \$38.8 trillion. Nonfinancial, or tangible, assets include real estate, equipment, inventories, and intellectual property products, and total \$48.8 trillion.

Nonfinancial corporations have assets valued at \$60.7 trillion, or 217.0 percent of GDP. These include nonfinancial assets (see\cbox{green!52!blue!55!white}) valued at 107.4 percent of GDP and financial assets (see\cbox{green!70!yellow!70!lightgray!90!white}) valued at 109.7 percent, as of 2023 Q4. Noncorporate business assets are valued at \$27.0 trillion, equivalent to 96.5 percent of GDP. Tangible assets (see\cbox{orange!50!red!45!white}) are equivalent to 67.2 percent of GDP, and include \$10.2 trillion in rental housing. Financial assets for the sector (see\cbox{orange!55!red}) total 29.3 percent of GDP.
