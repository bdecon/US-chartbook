Tangible, or non-financial, assets include peoples' homes as well as consumer durable goods, such as cars, furniture, and appliances. The market value of owner-occupied real estate is \$32.0 trillion in 2020 Q4, equivalent to 1.49 years of GDP (see\cbox{green!60!teal}). Consumer durable goods have a replacement value of \$6.2 trillion, or 0.29 years of GDP. Tangible assets are reported for the combined household and nonprofit sector and include real estate and equipment belonging to nonprofits, which totals \$4.4 trillion in 2020 Q4.
