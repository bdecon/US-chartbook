Tangible, or non-financial, assets include peoples' homes as well as consumer durable goods, such as cars, furniture, and appliances. The market value of owner-occupied real estate is \$41.2 trillion in 2022 Q2, equivalent to 1.63 years of GDP (see\cbox{green!60!teal}). Consumer durable goods have a replacement value of \$7.7 trillion, or 0.3 years of GDP. Tangible assets are reported for the combined household and nonprofit sector and include real estate and equipment belonging to nonprofits, which totals \$5.1 trillion in 2022 Q2.
