Government consumption and investment directly affect economic growth in the short-term. In the third quarter of 2022, government consumption spending and investment contributed 0.65 percentage point to the real GDP growth rate of 3.2 percent. Over the latest four quarters, government consumption and investment subtracted 0.05 percentage point to economic growth, on average. Since 1989, the average contribution has been 0.23 percentage points.
