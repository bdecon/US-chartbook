Government consumption and investment directly affect economic growth in the short-term. In the fourth quarter of 2021, government consumption spending and investment subtracted 0.45 percentage point from the real GDP growth rate of 7.0 percent. Over the latest four quarters, government consumption and investment contributed 0.03 percentage point to economic growth, on average. Since 1989, the average contribution has been 0.23 percentage points.
