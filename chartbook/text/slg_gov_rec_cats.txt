In 2024 Q1, combined state and local government tax receipts total 8.6 percent of GDP (see {\color{blue!60!black}\textbf{---}}), following nine percent of GDP one year prior. Since 2019, these tax receipts decreased by 0.3 percent of GDP. State and local government income tax receipts decreased by 0.2 percent of GDP over the same period.

Federal government transfers to state and local governments total 3.3 percent of GDP in 2024 Q1 (see {\color{orange!90!red}\textbf{---}}), and 3.6 percent one year prior. These transfers peaked during COVID-19 relief efforts, but have been climbing over time, from 1.6 percent of GDP in 1989. Other receipts are equivalent to 1.2 percent of GDP in 2024 Q1 (see {\color{black!30!white}\textbf{---}}).
