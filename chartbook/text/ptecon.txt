As of May 2021, 5,271,000 people are working part time because of economic reasons, equivalent to 3.3 percent of the labor force (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}), the highest level since March 2021 and slightly above the February 2020 rate of 2.7 percent. During the great recession, the involuntary part-time share of the labor force peaked at 6.0 percent in September 2010.
