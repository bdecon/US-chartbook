As of December 2021, 3,929,000 people are working part time because of economic reasons, equivalent to 2.4 percent of the labor force (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}), the lowest level since July 2019 and slightly below the February 2020 rate of 2.7 percent. During the great recession, the involuntary part-time share of the labor force peaked at 6.0 percent in September 2010.
