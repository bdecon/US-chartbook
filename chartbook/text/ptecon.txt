As of November 2021, 4,286,000 people are working part time because of economic reasons, equivalent to 2.6 percent of the labor force (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}), the lowest level since January 2020 and in line with the February 2020 rate of 2.7 percent. During the great recession, the involuntary part-time share of the labor force peaked at 6.0 percent in September 2010.
