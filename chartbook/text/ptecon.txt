As of April 2020, 10,887,000 people are working part time because of economic reasons, equivalent to 7.0 percent of the labor force (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}), the highest level in the data April 2020 and far above the February 2020 rate of 2.6 percent. During the great recession, the involuntary part-time share of the labor force peaked at 6.0 percent in September 2010.
