As of February 2022, BLS \href{https://www.bls.gov/webapps/legacy/cpsatab12.htm}{reports} that 0.67 percent of the age 16+ population have been unemployed for 27 weeks or longer, compared to 1.62 percent in February 2021 (see {\color{blue}\textbf{---}}). This measure of long-term unemployment peaked at 2.96 percent of the population in April 2010, but had fallen to 0.42 percent in December 2019. 
 
In February 2022, 1.00 percent of the age 16+ population have been unemployed for at least 15 weeks (see {\color{violet!90!black}\textbf{---}}), following 0.97 percent in January 2022, and 1.00 percent in December 2021.
