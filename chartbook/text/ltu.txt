As of November 2023, BLS \href{https://www.bls.gov/webapps/legacy/cpsatab12.htm}{reports} that 0.42 percent of the age 16 and older population have been unemployed for 27 weeks or longer, compared to 0.45 percent in November 2022 (see {\color{blue}\textbf{---}}). This measure of \textbf{long-term unemployment} peaked at 2.96 percent of the population in April 2010, but had fallen to 0.42 percent in December 2019.

 In November 2023, 0.75 percent of those age 16 and older have been unemployed for at least 15 weeks (see {\color{violet!90!black}\textbf{---}}), following 0.75 percent in October and 0.85 percent in September.. One-year prior, in November 2022, 0.72 percent are unemployed for 15 weeks or more.
