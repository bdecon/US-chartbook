As of September 2021, BLS \href{https://www.bls.gov/webapps/legacy/cpsatab12.htm}{reports} that 1.03 percent of the age 16+ population have been unemployed for 27 weeks or longer, compared to 0.93 percent in September 2020 (see {\color{blue}\textbf{---}}). This measure of long-term unemployment peaked at 2.96 percent of the population in April 2010, but had fallen to 0.36 percent in April 2020. 
 
In September 2021, 1.37 percent of the age 16+ population are unemployed for at least 15 weeks, following 1.62 percent in August 2021, and 1.73 percent in July 2021.
