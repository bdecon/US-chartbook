As of January 2022, BLS \href{https://www.bls.gov/webapps/legacy/cpsatab12.htm}{reports} that 0.65 percent of the age 16+ population have been unemployed for 27 weeks or longer, compared to 1.56 percent in January 2021 (see {\color{blue}\textbf{---}}). This measure of long-term unemployment peaked at 2.96 percent of the population in April 2010, but had fallen to 0.36 percent in April 2020. 
 
In January 2022, 0.97 percent of the age 16+ population have been unemployed for at least 15 weeks (see {\color{red}\textbf{---}}), following 1.00 percent in December 2021, and 1.12 percent in November 2021.
