As of July 2020, BLS \href{https://www.bls.gov/webapps/legacy/cpsatab12.htm}{reports} that 0.60 percent of the age 16+ population have been unemployed for 27 weeks or longer, compared to 0.48 percent in July 2019 (see {\color{blue}\textbf{---}}). This measure of long-term unemployment peaked at 2.96 percent of the population in April 2010, but had fallen to 0.36 percent in April 2020.\\ 
 
In July 2020, 3.03 percent of the age 16+ population are unemployed for at least 15 weeks, following 1.21 percent in June 2020, and 0.88 percent in May 2020.
