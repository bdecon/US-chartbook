As of March 2023, BLS \href{https://www.bls.gov/webapps/legacy/cpsatab12.htm}{reports} that 0.44 percent of the age 16 and older population have been unemployed for 27 weeks or longer, compared to 0.56 percent in March 2022 (see {\color{blue}\textbf{---}}). This measure of \textbf{long-term unemployment} peaked at 2.96 percent of the population in April 2010, but had fallen to 0.42 percent in December 2019. 
 
In March 2023, 0.82 percent of those age 16 and older have been unemployed for at least 15 weeks (see {\color{violet!90!black}\textbf{---}}), following 0.79 percent in February 2023, and 0.77 percent in January 2023.
