The CPS civilian non-institutionalized population is 324 million in the year ending February 2020, with growth of 0.3 percent over the past year, though other Census population growth estimates are around 0.6 percent. By age, 22.7 percent are under the age of 18 and 16.4 percent are age 65 or older. In 1989, the US population was 244 million, with 26.3 percent under 18 and 12.0 percent 65 or older. The pre-retirement age (45--64) share of the population has increased to 25.5 percent in the year ending February 2020 from 18.9 percent in 1989.
