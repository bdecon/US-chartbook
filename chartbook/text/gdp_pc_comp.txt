Much of the increase in GDP over the past 30 years comes from consumer spending. Consumer spending (see {\color{yellow!45!orange}\textbf{---}}) is equivalent to \$56,984 per person in 2024 Q1, a price-adjusted increase of \$26,267 since 1989.

Gross private domestic investment (see {\color{blue!70!black}\textbf{---}}) is equivalent to \$14,880 per person in 2024 Q1. Government spending and investment (see {\color{cyan!60!white}\textbf{---}}) totals \$14,681 per person. The trade deficit, equivalent to \$2,528 per person, is subtracted, to capture only domestic production (see {\color{green!60!black}\textbf{---}}).
