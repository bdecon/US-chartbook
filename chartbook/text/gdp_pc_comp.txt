Much of the increase in real GDP per capita over the past 30 years comes from consumer spending. Domestic consumer spending (see {\color{yellow!65!orange}\textbf{---}}) is equivalent to \$45,525 per person in 2021 Q1, a price-adjusted increase of \$18,790 since 1989. Gross private domestic investment (see {\color{blue!70!black}\textbf{---}}) is equivalent to \$11,803 per person in 2021 Q1, and government spending and investment (see {\color{cyan!60!white}\textbf{---}}) totals \$11,923 per person. Net exports equivalent to \$2,603 per person are subtracted to reflect only domestic production (see {\color{green!60!black}\textbf{---}}).
