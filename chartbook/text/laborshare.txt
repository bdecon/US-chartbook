Over the year ending 2024 Q1, labor receives 69.5 percent of net domestic income (see {\color{blue!50!cyan}\textbf{---}}). Labor's share increased 1.3 percentage points over the past year. For context, one percent of net domestic income translates to \$212 billion per year, which is \$1,340 per worker. 

 Labor's share in the corporate sector is 74.3 percent percent in 2024 Q1 (see {\color{green!60!blue}\textbf{---}}). The corporate sector has well-defined accounting, which is useful for this analysis. The corporate labor share is currently 8.6 percentage points below its 30-year high of 83.0 percent in 1993 Q1.
