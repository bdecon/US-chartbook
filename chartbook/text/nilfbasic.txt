Nonparticipants aged 16 and older total 100.2 million in January 2022, and make up 38.1 percent of the age 16 or older population, compared to 36.8 percent in January 2020. About half of nonparticipants, and 19.1 percent of the population, are retirees in January 2022 (see\cbox{green!80!blue!72!black}), compared to 18.3 percent percent in January 2020 (see\cbox{green!80!blue!60!lime}).

Disability or illness keeps an additional 5.3 percent out of the labor force in January 2022, compared to 5.5 percent in January 2020. Students who are out of the labor force make up 5.8 percent in January 2022 and 5.8 percent in January 2020, while unpaid caregivers are 4.9 percent in January 2022 and 4.6 percent in January 2020.
