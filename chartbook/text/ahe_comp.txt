For production and nonsupervisory workers, the highest average hourly earnings in May 2024 are in the utilities industry (\$45.36), followed by the information industry (\$40.55), and the mining and logging industry (\$36.12). The lowest wage industries in the latest data, by average hourly earnings, are leisure and hospitality (\$19.51) and retail trade (\$20.92).

Over the past year, nine of the 12 industry groups have wage growth above the increase in prices indicated by the consumer price index (see {\color{orange!50!yellow}\textbf{---}}). The financial activities industry had the fastest nominal growth rate, at 6.5 percent, followed by 5.7 percent in manufacturing and 5.4 percent in mining and logging.
