For production and nonsupervisory workers, the highest average hourly earnings in December 2023 are in the utilities industry (\$45.28), followed by the information industry (\$40.37), and the mining and logging industry (\$35.31). The lowest wage industries in the latest data, by average hourly earnings, are leisure and hospitality (\$19.24) and retail trade (\$20.83).

Over the past year, 10 of the 12 industry groups have wage growth above the increase in prices indicated by the consumer price index (see {\color{orange!50!yellow}\textbf{---}}). The transportation and warehousing industry had the fastest nominal growth rate, at 7.1 percent, followed by 5.8 percent in manufacturing and 5.7 percent in mining and logging.
