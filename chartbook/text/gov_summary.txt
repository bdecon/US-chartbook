As an overview of the consolidated government's effect on GDP, government receipts are equivalent to 27.5 percent of GDP in 2024 Q1, compared to 27.6 percent in 2020 Q1. The vast majority of these receipts are taxes, including social insurance contributions.

 Government spending is equivalent to 33.8 percent of GDP in the latest data and 33.7 percent in 2019. This includes consumption expenditures, which are the government's operating costs, transfers and subsidies, and interest payments. These are covered in more detail in the following subsections.
