The ratio of US total wealth, excluding public lands, to GDP increased to 4.66 in 2019 Q1 from 3.56 in 1989 Q1. The same ratio for the market value of corporate equities (see\cbox{magenta!50!violet}) increased to a 1.87 multiple of GDP in 2019 Q1 from 0.56 in 1989 Q1. The market value of residential real estate (see\cbox{green!80!blue}) increased to 1.4 times GDP from 1.27 in 1989. The other category (see\cbox{cyan!35!white}), which includes tangible assets other than residential real estate less US financial obligations to the rest of the world, decreased to 1.39 from 1.72 in 1989.
