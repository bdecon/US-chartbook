In 2019 Q4, federal government expenditures total \$4.9 trillion, equivalent to 22.4 percent of GDP, and receipts total \$3.6 trillion, or 16.8 percent of GDP. The federal deficit was therefore \$1.2 trillion or 5.7 percent of GDP. Over the past three years, the ratio of expenditures to GDP increased by a total of 0.4 percentage point, and the ratio of receipts to GDP has decreased by a total of 1.6 percentage points, causing the deficit to widen by 2.0 percent of GDP.
