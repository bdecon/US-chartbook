From 1989 to 2011, imports of consumer goods excluding petroleum increased by the equivalent of 5.7 percent of domestic consumption of goods; petroleum and products imports increased by the equivalent of 6.3 percent; and all other goods increased by the equivalent of 6.4 percent. Since 2011, imports of consumer goods decreased by the equivalent of 3.0 percent of domestic goods demand; imports of petroleum and products decreased by the equivalent of 7.1 percent; and other imports decreased by the equivalent of 1.4 percent.
