In the second quarter of 2023, gross private business investment totals \$3,532 billion on a seasonally-adjusted annualized basis, equivalent to 13.2 percent of GDP (see {\color{blue!60!violet}\textbf{---}}). Private business investment in fixed assets totals \$3,596 billion, or 13.4 percent of GDP (see {\color{cyan!80!white}\textbf{---}}). Private business depreciation totals \$2,979 billion in the quarter, or 11.1 percent of GDP (see {\color{magenta}\textbf{---}}). As a result, net fixed investment is \$618 billion, or 2.3 percent of GDP (see {\color{green!80!blue}\textbf{---}}). 

 In 2019 Q4, prior to the COVID-19 pandemic, private business gross investment was \$2,880 billion. Since 2019 Q4, gross investment increased at an annual rate of six percent. Net fixed investment was \$654 billion in 2019 Q4, and decreased at an annual rate of 1.6 percent from 2019 Q4 to 2023 Q2, as growth of depreciation costs outpaced the increase in gross investment.
