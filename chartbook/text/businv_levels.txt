In the the first quarter of 2021, gross private business investment totals \$2,815 billion on a seasonally-adjusted annualized basis, equivalent to 12.8 percent of GDP (see {\color{blue!60!violet}\textbf{---}}). Private business investment in fixed assets totals \$2,954 billion, or 13.4 percent of GDP (see {\color{cyan!80!white}\textbf{---}}). Private business depreciation totals \$2,384 billion in the quarter, or 10.8 percent of GDP (see {\color{magenta}\textbf{---}}). As a result, net investment is \$431 billion, or 2.0 percent of GDP (see {\color{green!80!blue}\textbf{---}}). 

 In 2019 Q4, prior to the COVID-19 pandemic, private business gross investment was \$2,839 billion. Since 2019 Q4, annualized gross investment decreased 0.8 percent. Net investment was \$569 billion in 2019 Q4, and decreased 24.3 percent from 2019 Q4 to 2021 Q1, as gross investment fell while deprecation was relatively constant.
