Nonfarm payrolls increased by 223,000 in December 2022, following 256,000 jobs added in November, and 263,000 added in October (see \cbox{blue!60!purple}). Average payroll growth was 247,300 over these three months, slightly below the average of 366,000 during the previous three months. 

 During March and April 2020, the US lost a combined 22 million jobs. Since May 2020, a total of 23.2 million jobs have been added, equivalent to 105.6 percent of those lost.

To maintain a steady payroll employment rate with population growth, the US needed to add 130,000 jobs in December 2022. Pre-pandemic, in 2019, the US was adding an average of 164,000 jobs per month.
