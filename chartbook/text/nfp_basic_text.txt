Nonfarm payrolls increased by 236,000 in March 2023, following 326,000 jobs added in February, and 472,000 added in January (see \cbox{blue!60!purple}). Average payroll growth was 344,700 over these three months, slightly above the average of 284,300 during the previous three months. 

 During March and April 2020, the US lost a combined 22 million jobs. Since May 2020, a total of 25.1 million jobs have been added, equivalent to 114.6 percent of those lost.

To maintain a steady payroll employment rate with population growth, the US needed to add 137,000 jobs in March 2023. Pre-pandemic, in 2019, the US was adding an average of 163,200 jobs per month.
