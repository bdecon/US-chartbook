Nonfarm payrolls increased by 206,000 in June 2024, following 218,000 jobs added in May, and 108,000 added in April (see\cbox{blue!60!purple}). Average payroll growth was 177,300 over these three months, slightly below the average of 267,300 during the previous three months. 

 During March and April 2020, the US lost a combined 22 million jobs. Since May 2020, a total of 28.2 million jobs have been added, equivalent to 128.9 percent of those lost.

To maintain a steady payroll employment rate with population growth, the US needed to add 80,000 jobs in June 2024. Pre-pandemic, in 2019, the US was adding an average of 165,700 jobs per month.
