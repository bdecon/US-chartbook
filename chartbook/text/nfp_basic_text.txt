Nonfarm payrolls increased by 253,000 in April 2023, following 165,000 jobs added in March, and 248,000 added in February (see \cbox{blue!60!purple}). Average payroll growth was 222,000 over these three months, slightly below the average of 333,700 during the previous three months. 

 During March and April 2020, the US lost a combined 22 million jobs. Since May 2020, a total of 25.2 million jobs have been added, equivalent to 115.0 percent of those lost.

To maintain a steady payroll employment rate with population growth, the US needed to add 140,000 jobs in April 2023. Pre-pandemic, in 2019, the US was adding an average of 163,200 jobs per month.
