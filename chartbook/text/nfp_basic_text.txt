Nonfarm payrolls increased by 517,000 in January 2023, following 260,000 jobs added in December 2022, and 290,000 added in November 2022 (see \cbox{blue!60!purple}). Average payroll growth was 355,700 over these three months, in line with the average of 342,000 during the previous three months. 

 During March and April 2020, the US lost a combined 22 million jobs. Since May 2020, a total of 24.6 million jobs have been added, equivalent to 112.3 percent of those lost.

To maintain a steady payroll employment rate with population growth, the US needed to add 133,000 jobs in January 2023. Pre-pandemic, in 2019, the US was adding an average of 163,200 jobs per month.
