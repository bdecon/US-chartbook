Nonfarm payrolls increased by 199,000 in November 2023, following 150,000 jobs added in October, and 262,000 added in September (see\cbox{blue!60!purple}). Average payroll growth was 203,700 over these three months, in line with the average of 168,700 during the previous three months. 

 During March and April 2020, the US lost a combined 22 million jobs. Since May 2020, a total of 26.7 million jobs have been added, equivalent to 121.5 percent of those lost.

To maintain a steady payroll employment rate with population growth, the US needed to add 152,000 jobs in November 2023. Pre-pandemic, in 2019, the US was adding an average of 163,200 jobs per month.
