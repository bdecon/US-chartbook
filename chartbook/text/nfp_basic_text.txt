Nonfarm payrolls increased by 311,000 in February 2023, following 504,000 jobs added in January, and 239,000 added in December 2022 (see \cbox{blue!60!purple}). Average payroll growth was 351,300 over these three months, in line with the average of 321,300 during the previous three months. 

 During March and April 2020, the US lost a combined 22 million jobs. Since May 2020, a total of 24.9 million jobs have been added, equivalent to 113.6 percent of those lost.

To maintain a steady payroll employment rate with population growth, the US needed to add 135,000 jobs in February 2023. Pre-pandemic, in 2019, the US was adding an average of 163,200 jobs per month.
