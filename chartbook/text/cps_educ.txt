Over the year ending March 2023, 86.4 million people over the age of 25, or 38.2 percent of the total, have at least a bachelor's degree, with 33.1 million of those, or 14.6 percent of the total, holding an advanced degree such as a master's degree, medical or law degree, or PhD. An additional 56.7 million people have some college coursework but no degree or have an associate degree. A total of 63.5 million have a high school diploma but no college, while 19.7 million have no high school diploma.
