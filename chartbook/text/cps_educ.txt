Over the year ending May 2021, 84.0 million people over the age of 25, or 37.6 percent of the total, have at least a bachelor's degree, with 31.7 million of those, or 14.2 percent of the total, holding an advanced degree such as a master's degree, medical or law degree, or PhD. An additional 56.9 million people have some college coursework but no degree or have an associate degree. A total of 62.6 million have a high school diploma but no college, while 19.9 million have no high school diploma.
