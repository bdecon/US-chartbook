Over the year ending July 2020, 82.3 million people over the age of 25, or 37.0 percent of the total, have at least a bachelor's degree, with 31.0 million of those, or 14.0 percent of the total, holding an advanced degree such as a master's degree, medical or law degree, or PhD. An additional 57.7 million people have some college coursework but no degree or have an associate degree. A total of 61.9 million have a high school diploma but no college, while 20.4 million have no high school diploma.
