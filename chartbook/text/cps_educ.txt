Over the year ending March 2020, 80.5 million people over the age of 25, or 36.3 percent of the total, have at least a bachelor's degree, with 30.1 million of those, or 13.6 percent of the total, holding an advanced degree such as a master's degree, medical or law degree, or PhD. An additional 57.7 million people have some college coursework but no degree or have an associate degree. A total of 62.4 million have a high school diploma but no college, while 21.2 million have no high school diploma.
