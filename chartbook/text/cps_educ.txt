Over the year ending August 2022, 85.2 million people over the age of 25, or 37.8 percent of the total, have at least a bachelor's degree, with 32.3 million of those, or 14.3 percent of the total, holding an advanced degree such as a master's degree, medical or law degree, or PhD. An additional 56.4 million people have some college coursework but no degree or have an associate degree. A total of 64.0 million have a high school diploma but no college, while 19.8 million have no high school diploma.
