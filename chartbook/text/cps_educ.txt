Over the year ending September 2023, 87.3 million people over the age of 25, or 38.4 percent of the total, have at least a bachelor's degree, with 33.5 million of those, or 14.7 percent of the total, holding an advanced degree such as a master's degree, medical or law degree, or PhD. 

An additional 56.8 million people have some college coursework but no degree or have an associate degree. A total of 63.6 million have a high school diploma but no college, while 19.5 million have no high school diploma.
