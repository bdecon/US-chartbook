Over the year ending October 2023, 87.5 million people over the age of 25, or 38.5 percent of the total, have at least a bachelor's degree, with 33.6 million of those, or 14.8 percent of the total, holding an advanced degree such as a master's degree, medical or law degree, or PhD. 

An additional 56.8 million people have some college coursework but no degree or have an associate degree. A total of 63.6 million have a high school diploma but no college, while 19.4 million have no high school diploma.
